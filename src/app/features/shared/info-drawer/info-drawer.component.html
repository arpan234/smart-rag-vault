RAG High-Level Workflow

Retrieval-Augmented Generation bridges the gap between static LLM intelligence and dynamic local data. Here is the step-by-step pipeline running inside your browser:

1

Document Ingestion & Chunking

When a PDF or txt file is dropped, we load the raw text and split it into smaller overlapping segments (chunks) based on your chunkSize configuration.

Raw Document
Chunk #1 Overlap Chunk #2
2

Vector Embedding

We convert each text chunk into a high-dimensional mathematical coordinate vector. These coordinates represent semantic concepts. Similar sentences will be positioned close to each other in this virtual coordinate space.

"Machine learning processes data" ➔ [0.12, -0.49, 0.81, ...]
3

Vector DB Storage & Cosine Search

Vectors are cached locally in **IndexedDB**. When you enter a prompt, we convert your prompt into a vector and compare the mathematical angle (**Cosine Similarity**) between your prompt vector and all cached document vector chunks, pulling the top-K matches.

4

Context Synthesis & LLM Chat

We compile the retrieved paragraphs alongside your prompt inside a structural RAG template (context wrapping) and transmit the payload to the LLM (Gemini or OpenAI). The AI responds streams back factually correct citations.

The Core Mathematics

How do machines understand that "kitten" and "cat" are related? They use vector mathematics. This app operates under two distinct vector engines:

Comparing Embedding Engines

Local VSM (TF-IDF) Calculates exact word frequencies across vocabulary vectors. Runs 100% offline in JS, free, but struggles with semantic synonyms.
Neural API Embeddings Uses pre-trained transformer model APIs to output multi-thousand dimension dense vectors capturing high-level conceptual meaning.

Cosine Similarity Metric

Retrieval matches documents by computing the Cosine Similarity angle between your query vector $A$ and document vector $B$. By measuring the angle rather than the straight-line Euclidean distance, long files containing repetitive words aren't skewed.

Cosine Similarity Formula
cos(θ) = (A • B) / (||A|| ||B||)
Output scale ranges from 1.0 (perfect directional alignment) to 0.0 (perpendicular / no relation).

Interactive Glossary

TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) {{ expandedMathTerm() === 'tfidf' ? 'Hide' : 'Expand' }}

TF-IDF scores word importance in a document. **Term Frequency (TF)** measures how often a word appears in a chunk. **Inverse Document Frequency (IDF)** offsets this by how common that word is across all documents, highlighting unique keywords while discounting filler terms (like "and", "the").

Dense vs Sparse Vectors {{ expandedMathTerm() === 'dense' ? 'Hide' : 'Expand' }}

**Sparse vectors** (like local TF-IDF) have dimensions equal to the entire vocabulary length, with values mostly being zero. They match exact keywords. **Dense vectors** (like OpenAI/Gemini Embeddings) are fixed lengths (768 or 1536 dimensions) filled with non-zero decimals. They match conceptual patterns even when exact keywords don't overlap (e.g., matching "puppy" to "young dog").

Vector Dot Product (A • B) {{ expandedMathTerm() === 'dot' ? 'Hide' : 'Expand' }}

The Dot Product multiplies matching indices of two vectors and sums them up: `(A1*B1) + (A2*B2) + ...`. If coordinates align in positive directions, the dot product is high; if they oppose or are orthogonal, it is zero or negative.
